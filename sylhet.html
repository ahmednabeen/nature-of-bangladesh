Welcome To Sylhet

Sylhet

the "Land of Tea Gardens" and "Spiritual Haven" of Bangladesh, is a captivating destination nestled in the lush hills of the northeastern region. Known for its sprawling tea estates, pristine natural landscapes, and revered shrines, Sylhet offers a serene yet vibrant charm. The city is enriched with cultural landmarks like the sacred Hazrat Shah Jalal Mazar Sharif, the stunning Jaflong with its crystal-clear riverbeds, and the mystical beauty of the Ratargul Swamp Forest. Its rolling hills, cascading waterfalls, and tranquil countryside create a picturesque retreat for visitors. The Surma River, winding through the city, provides breathtaking views and opportunities for peaceful boat rides. Famous for its tea, honey, and vibrant Sylheti culture, Sylhet entices visitors with its unique blend of spirituality, nature, and warm hospitality, making it an unforgettable destination in Bangladesh.

Hazrat Shah Jalal Mazar

Hazrat Shah Jalal Mazar, located in Sylhet City, Sylhet Division, Bangladesh, is one of the most revered religious sites in the country. The mausoleum houses the tomb of Hazrat Shah Jalal (R.A.), a 14th-century Sufi saint who played a pivotal role in spreading Islam in Bengal. Known for his piety and spiritual teachings, Shah Jalal's influence extended across the region, earning him widespread respect. The mazar complex features a mosque, a pond with sacred fish, and a serene courtyard that attracts pilgrims and visitors year-round. This historic site is a symbol of Sylhet's rich Islamic heritage and remains a center for spiritual reflection and cultural identity.

Jaflong

Jaflong, located in Gowainghat Upazila, Sylhet District, Sylhet Division, Bangladesh, is a breathtaking natural destination known for its stunning landscapes and serene ambiance. Nestled at the foothills of the Meghalaya range, Jaflong is famous for its crystal-clear rivers, rolling green hills, and stone collection activities. It is also home to the Khasia tribal community, adding a cultural dimension to its appeal. Historically, Jaflong has been a hub for cross-border trade due to its proximity to India. The area’s natural beauty, including the majestic Dawki River, has made it a popular spot for tourists seeking tranquility and adventure. Today, Jaflong remains one of Sylhet’s most iconic attractions.

Swam Forest

Swam Forest, located in Ranishongkail Upazila, Thakurgaon District, Rangpur Division, Bangladesh, is a unique and ecologically significant forest area. The forest spans a vast region of wetlands and swampy land, supporting diverse flora and fauna. Historically, Swam Forest has been crucial for maintaining the ecological balance in the region, acting as a habitat for various wildlife species, including migratory birds. The area has also been a source of livelihood for local communities, who depend on its natural resources for fishing and farming. Today, Swam Forest is recognized for its environmental importance and attracts nature lovers and researchers interested in wetlands conservation and biodiversity.

Surma River

Surma River, located in Sylhet Division, Bangladesh, is one of the major rivers in the northeastern region of the country. Originating from the Indian state of Meghalaya, the river flows through Sylhet District, playing a crucial role in the region's agriculture and transportation. Historically, the Surma River has been vital for trade and communication, especially during the British colonial period. The river is known for its clear waters and scenic beauty, attracting tourists and nature enthusiasts. It also supports the local economy by providing water for irrigation and serving as a major fishing ground. Today, Surma River remains an integral part of the cultural and ecological landscape of Sylhet.

Shah Poran Mazar

Shah Poran Mazar, located in Sylhet City, Sylhet Division, Bangladesh, is a revered Islamic shrine dedicated to Hazrat Shah Poran (R.A.), the nephew of Hazrat Shah Jalal (R.A.). He was a prominent Sufi saint and played a significant role in the spread of Islam in Bengal during the 14th century. The mazar attracts numerous pilgrims from all over Bangladesh and beyond, who come to pay their respects and seek spiritual blessings. The site is known for its peaceful atmosphere and historical significance. Shah Poran Mazar is an essential part of Sylhet's rich Islamic heritage and remains a major center for religious gatherings and cultural activities.

Pangthumai Waterfal

Pangthumai Waterfall, located in Moulvibazar District, Sylhet Division, Bangladesh, is a stunning natural waterfall nestled in the scenic hills of the Khasi Tribal region. Situated near the Indian border, Pangthumai is known for its crystal-clear waters and breathtaking views. The waterfall is surrounded by lush greenery, making it a popular destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. The region has historical significance as it has long been home to the Khasi indigenous people, who have preserved their unique culture and traditions. Pangthumai Waterfall remains one of the hidden gems of Sylhet.

Kena Bridge

Kena Bridge, located in Naogaon District, Rajshahi Division, Bangladesh, is an iconic historical bridge known for its unique architectural design and cultural significance. Built during the British colonial period, the bridge spans the Chalan Beel, a vast wetland area in the region. Kena Bridge was constructed to improve transportation and connect remote areas, facilitating trade and communication. The bridge, made of stone and brick, reflects the engineering prowess of its time. Today, Kena Bridge stands as a symbol of the region's colonial heritage, attracting both history enthusiasts and travelers for its historical importance and picturesque setting.

Ali Amzad Clock

Ali Amzad Clock Bridge, located in Rajshahi City, Rajshahi Division, Bangladesh, is a historic and iconic landmark of the city. Built in 1937, this unique bridge combines a functional roadway with an ornamental clock tower at its center. The bridge was constructed by the renowned philanthropist Ali Amzad, who wanted to enhance the city’s infrastructure and add a symbol of elegance to the area. Over time, it became not only a vital transportation link but also a symbol of Rajshahi’s growth during the British colonial era. Today, Ali Amzad Clock Bridge remains a popular spot for locals and tourists.
