Khulna

a charming city in southern Bangladesh, is celebrated for its rich history and breathtaking natural beauty. Known as the gateway to the Sundarbans, the World's largest mangrove forest and home to the majestic Bengal Tiger, Khulna offers unparalleled access to this UNESCO World Heritage Site. The city itself is dotted with historical treasures like the ancient Shat Gambuj Mosque, a stunning 15th-century architectural marvel in nearby Bagerhat, and the serene Khan Jahan Ali's Tomb. The tranquil Rupsha River flows through Khulna, adding to its scenic charm, while its vibrant bazaars and fresh seafood reflect the regionâ€™s cultural essence. Khulna is also a hub of craftsmanship, known for its exquisite honey and unique handicrafts. With its lush green surroundings, historical wonders, and warm hospitality, Khulna offers a perfect blend of natural and cultural delights for visitors.
