Welcome To Dhaka

Dhaka

the vibrant capital of Bangladesh, is a city steeped in history and culture, often referred to as the "City of Mosques and Rickshaws." This bustling metropolis, situated on the banks of the Buriganga River, is a dynamic blend of tradition and modernity. The historical landmarks like the magnificent Lalbagh Fort, the serene Ahsan Manzil (Pink Palace), and the sacred Star Mosque reflect its rich heritage. Dhaka's colorful streets are adorned with art, bustling markets like New Market and Bashundhara City, and street food that tempts the taste buds. The city's modern skyline is punctuated by towering buildings and the iconic Hatirjheel, a serene urban lake. With its vibrant festivals, diverse architecture, and warm-hearted people, Dhaka offers a unique and unforgettable experience to visitors.

Ahsan Manjil

Ahsan Manzil, located on the banks of the Buriganga River in Dhaka, Bangladesh, is a historic palace and an iconic symbol of the city’s rich heritage. Built in the mid-19th century, it served as the residence of the Nawab of Dhaka. Originally constructed by Sheikh Enayet Ullah in the 17th century, it was later rebuilt and expanded by Nawab Abdul Ghani, who named it after his son, Khwaja Ahsanullah. The palace, featuring Indo-Saracenic architecture, is now a museum showcasing the Nawabs' legacy and Dhaka's colonial history, drawing countless visitors fascinated by its grandeur and historical significance.

Baytul Mukaram

Baytul Mukarram, located in the heart of Dhaka, Bangladesh, is the national mosque and a significant landmark of the country. Constructed in the 1960s, it was designed by architect Abdulhusein M. Thariani and reflects a blend of modern and Islamic architectural styles, inspired by the design of the Kaaba in Mecca. With a capacity to accommodate thousands of worshippers, it serves as a center for Islamic activities and religious gatherings. Its unique cubic structure and historical significance make it a notable symbol of Bangladesh's Islamic heritage. The mosque also features a large prayer hall and courtyard.

Dhaka University

Dhaka University, located in the heart of Dhaka, Bangladesh, is the country's premier institution of higher education and a historic center for academic excellence. Established on July 1, 1921, it was founded during British colonial rule to meet the growing demand for advanced education in Bengal. Known as the "Oxford of the East," the university played a pivotal role in Bangladesh's socio-political history, including the Language Movement and the Liberation War. Its lush green campus spans 600 acres and houses numerous faculties, research centers, and iconic buildings such as Curzon Hall. Dhaka University continues to shape the nation's intellectual and cultural landscape, attracting students from across the region.

Suhrawardy Udyan

Suhrawardy Udyan, located in the center of Dhaka, Bangladesh, is a historic national park and an important site in the country’s history. Originally known as the Ramna Racecourse during British colonial rule, it was renamed to honor Huseyn Shaheed Suhrawardy, a prominent political leader. This park holds immense significance as the site where the Father of the Nation, Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman, delivered his historic speech on March 7, 1971, inspiring the liberation movement. It is also where the Pakistani forces surrendered on December 16, 1971, marking Bangladesh’s independence. Today, it serves as a peaceful green space and a reminder of the nation's freedom struggle.

Lalbag Fort

Lalbagh Fort, situated in the southwestern part of Old Dhaka, Bangladesh, is an incomplete but historically significant Mughal fort complex. Construction began in 1678 under the direction of Prince Muhammad Azam, the third son of Emperor Aurangzeb, during his tenure as the Subahdar of Bengal. However, the project remained unfinished due to Azam's recall to Delhi and the death of Pari Bibi, believed to be Azam's beloved, whose mausoleum lies within the fort. The complex features a mosque, Diwan-i-Aam, and lush gardens. Lalbagh Fort stands as a remarkable example of Mughal architecture and a cherished relic of Dhaka's historical past.

National Botanical Garden

The National Botanical Garden, located in Mirpur, Dhaka, Bangladesh, is the largest botanical garden in the country, spanning over 84 hectares. Established in 1961, it serves as a center for conservation, research, and recreation. The garden is home to an impressive collection of over 50,000 plant species, including rare tropical plants, medicinal herbs, and exotic trees. Divided into several sections, it features a serene lake, greenhouses, and a cactus house. The garden is an important resource for botanists and nature enthusiasts, offering a peaceful escape from the city's bustle while promoting the study of Bangladesh's rich biodiversity.

Jamuna Future Park

Jamuna Future Park, located in Bashundhara Residential Area, Dhaka, Bangladesh, is one of the largest shopping malls in South Asia. Opened in September 2013, it was developed by the Jamuna Group and spans over 4.1 million square feet. The mall features a modern architectural design, offering a wide range of facilities, including retail stores, a multiplex cinema, food courts, and an amusement zone. It serves as a one-stop destination for shopping, dining, and entertainment, attracting thousands of visitors daily. Jamuna Future Park has become a prominent commercial and recreational landmark in Bangladesh's capital city.

Bangabandhu safari park Gazipur

Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujib Safari Park, located in Gazipur, Bangladesh, is a renowned wildlife sanctuary and a major tourist attraction. Established in 2013, the park spans over 3,810 acres and is divided into several zones, including a core safari area, aviary, and herbivore and carnivore zones. Named after the Father of the Nation, Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman, the park aims to conserve wildlife and promote eco-tourism. Visitors can experience close encounters with animals such as tigers, lions, zebras, and deer in a natural habitat. The park also features a serene lake, nature trails, and educational programs.
