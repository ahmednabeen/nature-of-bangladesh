Welcome To Chattogram

Chattogram

the "Port City" and "Gateway to the Bay" of Bangladesh, is a bustling coastal metropolis brimming with natural beauty and cultural significance. Renowned for its scenic hills, expansive beaches, and dynamic seaport, Chattogram offers a perfect blend of urban vibrancy and tranquil retreats. The city boasts iconic landmarks like the serene Patenga Beach, the majestic Sitakunda Ecopark, and the historic World War II Cemetery. Adventurers can explore the breathtaking Boga Lake, the dramatic Nafakhum Waterfall, and the lush greenery of the Kaptai Lake. As the heart of Bangladesh's trade and commerce, Chattogram is also rich in cultural heritage, with its centuries-old temples, vibrant markets, and unique Chittagonian cuisine. Whether it's savoring fresh seafood by the Bay, delving into its history, or soaking in its natural splendor, Chattogram promises an enchanting experience that captivates every traveler.

Rangamati Hanging Bridge

Rangamati Hanging Bridge, located in Rangamati District, Chattogram Division, Bangladesh, is a picturesque and historic suspension bridge that spans the serene Kaptai Lake. The bridge was constructed in 1960 to provide better connectivity between the hill tracts of Rangamati and the surrounding areas. Known for its unique design, the bridge offers a thrilling experience as it sways gently over the lake, providing stunning views of the surrounding hills and water. It has become a popular tourist attraction, drawing visitors with its natural beauty and historical significance. Today, the Rangamati Hanging Bridge is an important part of the region's infrastructure and a symbol of local engineering innovation.

Chattogram Foys Lake

Foy's Lake Bridge, located in Chattogram City, Chattogram Division, Bangladesh, is an iconic bridge that spans the picturesque Foy's Lake. Built in 1924, the bridge was constructed as part of the development of Foy’s Lake, a man-made lake initially created for water supply purposes. The bridge, named after its founder, Sir A. H. Foy, serves as both a functional piece of infrastructure and a beautiful landmark. It connects the surrounding hills with the lake, providing visitors with scenic views of the area. Today, Foy’s Lake and its bridge are popular tourist destinations, attracting nature lovers and history enthusiasts alike.

Chattogram Port

Chattogram Port, located in Chattogram City, Chattogram Division, Bangladesh, is the busiest seaport in the country and one of the most significant ports in South Asia. Established in 1887, it has played a crucial role in Bangladesh’s trade and economic development, serving as the gateway for the majority of the country’s imports and exports. The port’s strategic location on the Bay of Bengal has made it a key hub for maritime trade. Over the years, Chattogram Port has undergone extensive modernization, including the expansion of its container terminals and infrastructure. Today, it remains an essential component of Bangladesh's economy, supporting global trade and commerce.

Sitakunda Ecopark

Sitakunda Ecopark, located in Sitakunda Upazila, Chattogram District, Chattogram Division, Bangladesh, is a popular eco-tourism destination known for its natural beauty and rich biodiversity. The park covers a vast area of hills, forests, and wildlife, making it an ideal spot for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. Established in 2005, the park was created to preserve the region’s unique flora and fauna while promoting sustainable tourism. Sitakunda Ecopark is home to various species of birds, plants, and animals, as well as several trekking trails offering panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes. Today, it remains a cherished destination for eco-tourism in Bangladesh.

World War 2 Cemetery

World War 2 Cemetery, located in Chittagong City, Chattogram Division, Bangladesh, is a solemn memorial dedicated to the soldiers who lost their lives during World War II. The cemetery is the final resting place for Commonwealth military personnel, primarily from the United Kingdom, India, and other Allied nations, who died in the region during the war. Established in 1947, the cemetery is maintained by the Commonwealth War Graves Commission and contains hundreds of graves. The site serves as a reminder of the sacrifices made during the war and stands as a symbol of international peace and remembrance. Today, it remains a significant historical and cultural landmark in Chattogram.

Nafakhum Waterfal Bandarban

Nafakhum Waterfall, located in Ruma Upazila, Bandarban District, Chattogram Division, Bangladesh, is the largest waterfall in Bangladesh and one of the most stunning natural attractions in the region. With a height of around 100 feet, the waterfall cascades down the hills of the Chittagong Hill Tracts, surrounded by lush greenery and the rugged beauty of the hill landscape. Nafakhum is part of the indigenous Khasi community's land and has historical significance as a cultural and spiritual site. The waterfall’s remote location makes it a popular destination for trekkers and nature enthusiasts. Today, Nafakhum Waterfall stands as a symbol of Bangladesh’s natural beauty and eco-tourism potential.

Kaptai Lake

Kaptai Lake, located in Kaptai Upazila, Rangamati District, Chattogram Division, Bangladesh, is the largest man-made lake in the country. Created in 1960 by the construction of the Kaptai Dam on the Karnaphuli River, the lake was initially developed for hydroelectric power generation and irrigation purposes. Spanning over 6800 hectares, Kaptai Lake is known for its scenic beauty, surrounded by lush hills and forests. The lake has become a popular tourist destination, offering boating, fishing, and hiking opportunities. It also plays a vital role in the local economy, supporting both the fishing industry and eco-tourism in the Chittagong Hill Tracts region.

Golden Tample Bandarban

Golden Temple, located in Bandarban District, Chattogram Division, Bangladesh, is a revered Buddhist temple and an important pilgrimage site. Officially known as Kyangchhara Buddhist Temple, it is famous for its stunning golden pagoda that stands as a symbol of Buddhist faith and culture in the region. The temple is situated on a hilltop, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys and hills. Built in the 1990s, the Golden Temple is not only a place of worship but also a cultural landmark, reflecting the rich Buddhist heritage of the Chittagong Hill Tracts. Today, it attracts both local devotees and international visitors, drawn to its spiritual significance and natural beauty.

Cox's Bazar Beach

Cox's Bazar Beach, located in Cox's Bazar District, Chattogram Division, Bangladesh, is renowned for being the longest natural sea beach in the world, stretching over 120 kilometers. The beach, with its golden sands and tranquil waters, has long been a popular tourist destination, attracting millions of visitors annually. Historically, it became a prominent spot during the British colonial period when it was used for trade and as a naval base. Today, Cox’s Bazar Beach is not only a hub for tourism but also an important cultural and economic center for the region. Its stunning beauty, along with nearby attractions like Himchari and Inani Beach, make it a world-famous coastal destination.

Saint Martin Island

Saint Martin's Island, located in the Bay of Bengal, near the southernmost tip of Bangladesh, is the country’s only coral island and a popular tourist destination. Situated about 9 kilometers off the coast of Teknaf Upazila in Cox's Bazar District, Chattogram Division, the island is known for its crystal-clear waters, vibrant coral reefs, and rich marine life. Saint Martin's Island has a history dating back to the 15th century, with the name "Narical Gingira" (Coconut Island) being used by local fishermen. Over time, it became a significant spot for trade and tourism. Today, it attracts visitors with its natural beauty, tranquil atmosphere, and diverse wildlife.
